Fatal Plane Crashes Down Under

Whenever someone feels afraid to board a plane for the first time, they are often reassured by the fact that "planes are the safest mode of transport in the world", and that is correct! In fact, you are 40 times more likely to die in a car accident, and 3 times more likely to die in a train accident. So for the very few fatal plane accidents that do occur, what causes them? Where do they happen and how many have occured? Let's dive into the plane accidents that have occured in Australia in the past century and find out!

Whereabouts in Australia do fatal plane crashes occur?

14% of fatal plane crashes in Australia were due to bad weather or poor visibility.

13% of fatal plane crashes in Australia were due to engine failure.

2% of fatal plane crashes in Australia were due to being shot down by Japanese military aircrafts.

When do plane crashes occur in Australia?

The safest time to be on a plane is 12:00AM in the morning as it is the time with the least crashes, although this might also be because fewer planes fly at that time. The time with the most crashes is 12PM at noon.

Most of the plane crashes occurred during the period of World War II. 1942 was the year with the most plane crashes. 5 fatal plane crashes occured, causing 48 deaths in total. Crashes have significantly decreased in the later years, which gives reassurance about the safety of modern planes.

How Does Australia Compare to The Rest of The World?

There are much fewer crashes in Australia than other countries in the world. USA and Europe are the places with the most crashes in the world.
